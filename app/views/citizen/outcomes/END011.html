You have the right to apply for benefits in the UK

Based on your answers, as a citizen of a European Economic Area country with a job in the UK you should have the right to apply for benefits in the UK.

What to do next

Go to the jobcentre on the date of your habitual residency interview and bring the following items with you:

  • your passport or national ID card
  • proof of employment and how much you earn (eg recent wage slips or P60) or an offer of employment if you’ve not started working yet
  • proof of your partner’s nationality
  • details of your partner’s work, if they have a job
  • your marriage certificate, proof of civil partnership or 2 years’ proof of relationship

You will then have a short interview about whether you live and intend to stay in the UK. This will cover where you live, when you arrived and details of your daily life, for example your doctor or dentist.

If you have other family in the UK

You’ll be asked questions about your children or any other family you have in the UK. This will include the dates they arrived, the school they go to and the work they do.
