You should be able to apply for benefits in the UK

You have the right to apply for benefits in the UK as long as you can prove this.

What to do next

Go to the jobcentre on the date of your habitual residency interview and bring the following items with you:

  • your visa with the stamp or information saying you can apply for benefits (this may say ‘recourse to public funds’, or similar)
  • evidence of where you live

You will have a short interview about whether you live and intend to stay in the UK. This will cover where you live, when you arrived and details of your daily life, for example your doctor or dentist.

If you have other family in the UK

You’ll also be asked questions about your children or any other family you have in the UK. This will include the dates they arrived, the school they go to and the work they do.
