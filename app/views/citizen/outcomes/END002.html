Don't do a HRT with this person

They're an employed worker from a country in the European Economic Area. This means they can apply for benefits as long as they can prove this.

Evidence you need

  • passport
  • 3 months' proof of employment that shows how much they earn, such as recent wage slips, P60, employment contract or bank statement
  • proof of marriage, civil partnership or relationship if they have a partner

What to do next

  1. Open the HRT entitlement action to-do
  2. In the agent notes box, type in details of the evidence you've collected such as:
    • passport number, date of issue and expiry date
    • name of employer and amount of earnings shown on payslips, including dates
    • contract of employment if they've not yet started work (including type of contract, duration of contract, name of employer, hours of work and earnings)
    • bank statement showing earnings and dates
  3. Download HRT entitlement action ALP and in the final box, type in ‘HRT trial’ (leave the other boxes blank)
  4. After you've saved and uploaded the pdf, close the HRT entitlement action to-do

    5. Don't create agent ‘refer to decision maker (HRT)’ to-do

  5. Email the decision maker at stockport.pyca@dwp.gsi.gov.uk (give the full name of the person claiming)
  6. Remind the person claiming to check their Universal Credit account later for a decision
