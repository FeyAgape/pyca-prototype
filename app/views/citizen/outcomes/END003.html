You may be able to apply for benefits in the UK

You don’t have the right to apply for benefits in the UK yourself, but you may be able to through your partner.

What to do next

Go to the jobcentre on the date of your habitual residency interview and bring the following items with you:

  • your passport, identity card or biometric residence permit (BRP)
  • proof of employment if you work (wage slip or similar)
  • your partner’s passport or biometric residence permit (BRP) card
  • proof of your partner’s employment (wage slip or similar)
  • proof of your marriage, civil partnership or relationship
  • proof of dates both of you arrived in the country

You will have a short interview about whether you live and intend to stay in the UK. This will cover where you live, when you arrived and details of your daily life, for example your doctor or dentist.
