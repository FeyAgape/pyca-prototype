You can apply for benefits in the UK

Based on your answers you're a single, employed worker from a country in the European Economic Area. This means you can apply for benefits as long as you can prove this.

You now need to bring the following items to a jobcentre:

  • your passport
  • some proof of your employment that shows how much you earn, eg wage slips or employment contract
  • some proof of where you live, eg a rent agreement or mortgage statement dated in the last 3 months

What to do next

Arrange your identity interview at your local jobcentre (this is in your Universal Credit 'to-do' list).

When you come to your identity interview bring the evidence listed here (some of it is needed for your identity interview anyway), and mention that you need to prove your right to apply for benefits in the UK (this is often called 'habitual residency' by work coaches).

You might be asked to come back later if the jobcentre is busy, otherwise you should be able to prove your right to apply straight away.

At your appointment your evidence will simply be photocopied - it should take no more than 5 minutes.

Save and return to Universal Credit

You'll be able to see this information again from your Universal Credit 'to-do' at any time.
